# Beyond-Unitary-Designs-Quantifying-Trainability-in-Parameterized-Quantum-Circuits
Measure-based framework for analyzing trainability and gradient variance in parameterized quantum circuits beyond unitary design assumptions.


## Abstract
Parameterized quantum circuits (PQCs) represent a central class of controllable quantum evolutions, yet their trainability can be severely limited by exponentially vanishing gradients, known as the barren plateau phenomenon. Existing analyses typically rely on strong randomness assumptions, such as unitary designs, and remain limited in addressing general structured circuits. In this work, we develop a measure-based framework to characterize the trainability of general PQCs and establish a level classification of gradient variance across orthogonal subspaces. Our results reveal a trainability advantage in previously unexplored unitary 1-designs compared to higher-order designs. We further introduce the notion of improvement potential to quantify maximal variance enhancement under local disturbances. This framework provides unified analytical guidance for understanding and engineering trainable quantum circuits on near-term quantum hardware.

## Key Contributions
- A **measure-based framework** for analyzing PQC trainability beyond unitary design assumptions  
- A **level classification** of gradient variance via orthogonal decomposition  
- Identification of a **trainability advantage of unitary 1-designs**  
- Introduction of **improvement potential** to quantify variance enhancement under perturbations
